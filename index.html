Common Songbirds of North America

The American Crow

The American Crow is a large, long-legged, thick-necked bird with a heavy, straight bill. In flight, the wings are fairly broad and rounded with the wingtip feathers spread like fingers. The short tail is rounded or squared off at the end.

The American Robin

The American Robin is a small, brown bird with an orange stomach. Robin eggs are light blue and robins have up to three broods each year.

The European Starling

The European Starling is actually an invasive species in North America. They are easily identified by their shiny coats and shrill chirps.

The Blue Jay

The Blue Jay has a striking pattern with various shades of blue. It is difficult to tell the males and females apart with one glance.
more about blue jays

The Northern Cardinal

The Northern Cardinal is the state bird for 7 states in the USA. Their call is arguably one of the more musical ones and has inspired many songs over the years.
